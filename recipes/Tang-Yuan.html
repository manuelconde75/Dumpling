Tang Yuan (Sweet Dumplings)

Celebrate sweetness and tradition with Tang Yuan, a Chinese dessert full of cultural significance. These soft glutinous rice balls, filled with black sesame or peanut paste, are served in a sweet ginger broth that comforts the soul. Perfect for special occasions like the Lantern Festival, Tang Yuan symbolize unity and harmony. Learn how to prepare them and share this warm and love-filled dessert with your family.

! I love Dumpling ♡!

~ Ingredients ~


  • 200 g of glutinous rice flour

  • 120 ml of hot water

  • 50 g of black sesame paste or sweet bean paste

  • 600 ml of water

  • 50 g of sugar

  • 2 slices of ginger (optional)


Step 1

In a bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour with the hot water until a smooth and manageable dough forms. Knead for about 5-8 minutes. Divide the dough into small portions the size of a marble. Flatten each portion and place a bit of filling in the center. Wrap the filling with the dough, seal well, and roll gently between your palms to form smooth balls.

Step 2

In a large pot, boil water and cook the tang yuan by immersing them directly. You will know they are ready when they float to the surface, which will take about 3-5 minutes. Carefully remove them using a slotted spoon and place them in a bowl.

Step 3

While the tang yuan are cooking, prepare the sweet soup. In a separate pot, boil the 600 ml of water with the sugar and ginger if using. Cook for 5 minutes to infuse the flavors. Serve the tang yuan in a bowl and pour the hot soup over them. Enjoy them freshly made.
