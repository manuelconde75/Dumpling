Xiao Long Bao (Steamed Dumplings)

These little treasures, carefully folded and steamed in a traditional bamboo steamer, combine the softness of their dough with a juicy and aromatic filling.

! I love Dumpling ♡!

~ Ingredients ~


  • 200 g of flour

  • 100 ml of hot water

  • a pinch of salt

  • ½ tbsp of sugar

  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce

  • 200 g of ground pork

  • 2 tbsp of chopped green onion

  • 1 tbsp of grated ginger

  • 1 tbsp of sesame oil

  • broth gelatin (optional, for the "soup")


Step 1

In a bowl, mix the flour with the salt and gradually add the hot water while mixing with chopsticks or a wooden spoon. Knead for about 8-10 minutes until you have a smooth and elastic dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes. Divide the dough into small balls the size of a walnut and roll them out into thin circles with a rolling pin.

Step 2

For the filling, mix in a bowl the ground pork with the chopped green onion, grated ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. If using broth gelatin, chop it into small cubes and add it to the filling to create the "soup" effect. Place a teaspoon of filling in the center of each dough circle and close it by forming small pleats until sealed, like a little pouch.

Step 3

Place the dumplings in a steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Steam for 8-10 minutes over boiling water. Once ready, serve hot with soy sauce mixed with rice vinegar and grated ginger.
