Gyoza (Fried Dumplings)

Let yourself be seduced by the irresistible contrast of textures and flavors of Gyoza, the most famous Japanese dumplings. With a golden and crispy layer on the outside, and a juicy and well-seasoned filling on the inside, gyoza are the perfect balance between fried and tender.

! I love Dumpling ♡!

~ Ingredients ~


  • 200 g of flour

  • 100 ml of hot water

  • a pinch of salt

  • 1 tbsp of sesame oil

  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce

  • 150 g of ground pork

  • 50 g of shredded cabbage

  • 1 clove of minced garlic

  • 1 tbsp of grated ginger


Step 1

Prepare the dough by mixing the flour with the salt and gradually adding the hot water. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth. Let it rest covered for 30 minutes. Then, divide into small balls and roll them out into thin circles about 8 cm in diameter. If you prefer, you can use ready-made dumpling wrappers.

Step 2

In a bowl, mix the ground pork with the shredded cabbage, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil until the filling is well combined. Place a tablespoon of filling in the center of each dough circle, fold them in half, and seal the edges by pressing with your fingers or making small pleats for a decorative finish.

Step 3

In a large skillet, heat a little oil over medium heat. Place the gyoza with the base down and fry until golden. Then, add a splash of water (about ¼ cup), cover, and let them steam for about 3-4 minutes until the water evaporates. Serve with soy sauce and a few drops of chili oil or rice vinegar.
